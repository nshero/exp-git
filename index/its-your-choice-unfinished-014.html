it's your choice (Unfinished) (014)

"You know, Dad, it's your choice about the hospice thing," I said to him the morning I left before my aunt arrived to give me a ride back through the mountains to the city. "I know Mom's being kinda pushy."

He sat in one of the green reclining chairs and nodded. "Yeah, she's being a little pushy." He scratched his brow with a slightly shaking hand, now unsteady as his heart disease progressed further and further. He looked at the television instead of me. I like to think he knew I preferred it this way for this kind of conversation, feeling less like he was ignoring me and more like he was sparing me. Maybe some part of his decaying brain and heart could understand how closeness was pain, how love is simply the other side of loss.

"I know, but it's your life, you know? You're the only one who can make the decision," I said. "I just want you guys to have more help."
