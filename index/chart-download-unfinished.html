chart download (unfinished)

I like to let myself roam freely with the archive project, especially when I'm doing the things I don't want to do. Everyone asks: Why save the things you don't want to save? I wish I knew. I have to save my father's medical records because... because this was one of the ways I checked in on my parents? Because I mostly viewed my father as his illness (both unfair and unsurprising)?

I find a rhythm, just like I used to do for assembling patient packets or making copies or data entry. Open, open, close, close. Download every encounter that Dad had with the medical system that they'll let me access online. Save the contextless numbers and abbreviations recording the tumultuous decline of a human body, pages of lab results that feel as empty and inscrutable as the reasons for doing this weird shit.

I alternate between letting the shutters of my heart hang open in pain and feeling the surprising calm of roteness fall over me as I move backwards in time. Open, open, close, close. Maybe I should try to rush through some of these more acutely painful activities like ripping off a band-aid. Work to focus intently on this part no matter how difficult it feels, no matter how much my heart is itching to get up and walk around to protect itself. Because there are still a lot of lab results to download and it can't take forever. Or can it?

Open, open, close, close. The number of days between progress notes grows larger as I rewind, opening into weeks and months of gaps. That was the time when Dad was living his life, I remind myself. I imagine him washing the car in the driveway, radio set to WKYE 95.5FM.
